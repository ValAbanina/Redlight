FAQs

Find answers to your most pressing questions about our Red Light Therapy devices.

What is Red Light Therapy?

Red Light Therapy is a non-invasive treatment that uses low-level red light wavelengths (typically 660nm to 850nm) to penetrate the skin and stimulate cellular energy production. This process, known as photobiomodulation, helps improve various health conditions and promotes overall wellness by enhancing mitochondrial function at the cellular level.

How does Red Light Therapy work?

Red light therapy works by emitting specific wavelengths of light that penetrate the skin and are absorbed by mitochondria in cells. This stimulates ATP (adenosine triphosphate) production, which is the energy currency of cells. Enhanced ATP production helps cells function more efficiently, promoting healing, reducing inflammation, and supporting various biological processes.

What are the benefits of Red Light Therapy?

Red light therapy offers numerous benefits including improved skin health and anti-aging effects, reduced inflammation and pain relief, enhanced muscle recovery and athletic performance, better sleep quality, increased energy levels, wound healing acceleration, and improved circulation. Scientific studies have shown its effectiveness for various conditions.

Is Red Light Therapy safe?

Yes, red light therapy is generally considered very safe when used properly. It's non-invasive, doesn't produce heat that can burn, and has no known serious side effects. However, it's important to follow manufacturer guidelines, use proper eye protection, and consult with a healthcare professional if you have specific medical conditions or are taking photosensitizing medications.

How often should I use Red Light Therapy?

The frequency of use depends on your specific goals and the device you're using. Generally, sessions can range from 3-7 times per week for 10-20 minutes each. For general wellness, 3-4 times per week is often sufficient. For specific conditions or recovery, daily use may be beneficial. It's best to start with shorter, less frequent sessions and gradually increase as your body adapts.

What's the difference between your therapy beds and panels?

Our therapy beds like the Mito Booster XL provide full-body treatment with 6 different wavelengths and are designed for professional use or serious home users. Panels are more targeted, portable, and cost-effective for specific areas. Beds offer comprehensive coverage and multiple wavelengths, while panels are perfect for targeted treatment of specific body parts.

Which wavelengths do your devices use?

Our devices use scientifically-proven wavelengths including 660nm (red light) for surface-level benefits like skin health, and 850nm (near-infrared) for deeper tissue penetration. Our premium Mito Booster XL features 6 different wavelengths: 630nm, 660nm, 810nm, 830nm, 850nm, and 940nm for comprehensive therapeutic benefits.

How do I set up my red light device?

Setup varies by device. Panels typically require mounting to a wall or stand and plugging into a standard outlet. Therapy beds require professional installation which we provide. All devices come with detailed setup instructions and our customer support team is available to help with any questions during installation.

What power requirements do your devices have?

Most of our panels work with standard 110V household outlets. Larger devices like therapy beds may require 220V outlets. Power consumption varies from 100W for smaller panels to 1500W+ for full-body beds. All devices are designed for energy efficiency and include power specifications in the product descriptions.

How long do the LEDs last?

Our high-quality LEDs are rated for 50,000+ hours of operation, which translates to many years of regular use. We use only premium-grade LEDs that maintain their output over time. All devices come with warranties that cover LED performance, and our customer service team can assist with any LED-related issues.

Are there any contraindications for red light therapy?

While generally safe, red light therapy should be avoided over cancerous lesions, during pregnancy (especially over the abdomen), with certain photosensitizing medications, and by those with specific eye conditions. Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new therapy, especially if you have medical conditions or take medications.

Do I need to wear eye protection?

Yes, we highly recommend wearing the provided safety glasses when using red light devices, especially when treating the face or neck area. While red light is generally safe for eyes, prolonged direct exposure to bright LED lights can cause discomfort. Eye protection is included with all our devices.

Can I use red light therapy with other treatments?

Red light therapy can often complement other treatments and therapies. Many users combine it with exercise, physical therapy, skincare routines, and other wellness practices. However, always consult with your healthcare provider before combining treatments, especially if you're undergoing medical treatment or taking medications.
